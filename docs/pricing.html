The Economics: Dynamic Pricing v0.6.1

Storage on the edge is a finite resource. didbox402 uses a predictable pricing model to ensure the service pays for its own infrastructure while remaining accessible.

Pricing Formula

The cost is calculated based on volume (bytes) and duration (hours), with a 1MB minimum charge to cover indexing and D1 overhead.

Total Cost = Math.max(1MB, Size) * Hours * Base_Rate
  • Base_Rate: Configured by the node operator (e.g., 100 Satoshis per MB-Hour).
  • Currency: Satoshis (via the Lightning Network).
  • Minimum Charge: 1 MB-Hour.
  • Maximum Duration: 8,760 Hours (1 Year).
Dual-Rail Support: v0.6.1 supports both L402 (Standard Lightning) and x402 (Credit/Prepaid) rails.

Payment Specification

The payment flow follows the 402 standard with dual-rail support:

  1. Discovery: Client sends a request without an authorization header.
  2. Challenge: Server responds with 402 Payment Required.
    • For L402: WWW-Authenticate: L402 macaroon="{m}", invoice="{i}"
    • For x402: X-Invoice: {i}
  3. Settlement: Client pays the invoice via their preferred rail.
  4. Proof: Client retries the request:
    • For L402: Authorization: L402 {macaroon}:{preimage}
    • For x402: X-Payment: {preimage}

Egress-Aware Pricing

On infrastructure that charges for bandwidth (e.g., AWS S3), the node may require a Pay-to-Download x402 payment matching the object's size multiplied by the provider's egress rate.
